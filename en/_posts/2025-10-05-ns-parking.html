If I park in the red zone and then, before the hour expires, move to the blue zone, do I need to pay for the blue zone?

Below is correspondence with «JKP Parking Service - Novi Sad»

Can you clarify how parking payment works?

If a car is first parked in the red zone, and then, before the paid hour expires, I move it to the blue zone — do I need to pay separately for the blue zone or does the already paid time from the red zone still apply?

It would be helpful if you could provide the specific rule or a link to the document where this is specified.

⚠️ Read through to the end of the post

If you've paid for the red zone and the time hasn't expired yet, and then you move your car to the blue zone, you don't need to pay for the blue zone — the red zone is "stronger," and during inspection the system will see that payment exists.

Only when you've paid for the blue zone and then move to a higher priority zone (red or extra) — then the system won't recognize the payment, because you've paid for a weaker zone but are parked in a stronger one. In that case you need to pay for the zone where you're parked.

I understand, the system logic is clear. But formally speaking, this is a new parking instance in a different zone under a different sign. Can you provide an official public document that describes this specific case?

Since there was no response to my previous question — am I correct in understanding that there are no official rules or documents regulating this situation?

After checking with the Legal Department: you as a user are obligated to use public parking in accordance with traffic signs (meaning you're obligated to pay for parking). Management of public parking in the city is carried out by Public Utility Company "Parking Service" based on the DECISION ON MANAGEMENT OF PUBLIC PARKING LOTS IN THE TERRITORY OF THE CITY OF NOVI SAD ("Official Gazette of the City of Novi Sad", No. 20/2023, 35/2023 - authentic interpretation and 18/2024).
